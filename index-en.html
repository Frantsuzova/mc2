AIMs Seminar

The international AIMs seminar is held annually in June, with smaller meetups taking place throughout the year. Currently, in-person events are held in Saint Petersburg (Russia).

Communication between visitors and museums within exhibitions is closely tied to the art of storytelling. New approaches to storytelling are associated with the possibilities offered by multimedia and digital technologies. Their use in museum exhibitions provides visitors with a deeper interaction with the context embedded in the display and creates a personal connection with the presented material.

The goal of the seminar is to discuss the current state, prospects, and directions for the use of digital technologies in museums. Thus, the event serves as a platform for discussing the impact of digital technologies in the museum environment and includes two components — practical case studies and scientific analysis.

Our target audience includes exhibition curators, IT specialists producing digital and multimedia content for museums, art historians, and cultural scholars.

Key focus areas of the international seminar:

  • Scenography: challenges and methods for developing concepts of visual representation for exhibitions; working with lighting and spatial composition; color theory as it applies to exhibition design tasks.
  • Storytelling: choosing a narrative language; working with exhibits; coordination between curators and designers; challenges in effectively conveying information.
  • Interactive Journey: integrating interactive elements into exhibitions (AR, VR); creating routes and scenarios to engage visitors; developing interactive installations; creating visual and sound effects.
  • Visitor Experience: results of studies focused on analyzing visitor perception and the influence of scenography’s visual elements, as well as the effectiveness of routes and scenarios.

Follow our announcements and let’s rethink together how technology can serve art, opening new horizons for interaction between museums and their visitors.
