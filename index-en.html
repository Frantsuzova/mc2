AIMs Seminar

The international AIMs seminar is held annually in June, with smaller meetups taking place throughout the year. Currently, the location for in-person events is Saint Petersburg (Russia).

The international seminar "Art and Innovations in Museums" (AIMs 2025) is dedicated to the challenges of integrating digital technologies into the museum environment, and consequently, to the changes in the nature and strategies of interaction between museums and their visitors. The seminar focuses on discussing new approaches and technological solutions. Their use in museum exhibitions provides visitors with deeper interaction with the context embedded in the display and creates a personal connection with the presented material.

The seminar's goal is to discuss the current state, prospects, and directions for the use of digital technologies in museums. Thus, the event serves as a platform for discussing the impact of digital technologies in the museum environment and includes two components — practical case studies and scientific analysis.

Our target audience includes exhibition curators, IT specialists producing digital and multimedia content for museums, art historians, and cultural scholars.

Key focus areas of the international seminar:

  • Scenography: challenges and methods for developing concepts of visual representation for exhibitions; working with lighting and spatial composition; color theory within the tasks of exhibition design.
  • Storytelling: choosing a narrative language; working with exhibits; coordination between curators and designers; challenges in effectively conveying information.
  • Interactive Journey: integrating interactive elements into exhibitions (AR, VR); creating routes and scenarios to engage visitors; developing interactive installations; creating visual and sound effects.
  • Interactive Interfaces: designing and implementing applications and interactive panels that provide new ways for visitors to engage with museum content.
  • Audio Design: creating audio guides, sound installations, and interactive audio elements that guide and accompany visitors through the museum space.
  • Data Analysis and Machine Learning: using machine learning in the creation of interactive exhibit elements.
  • Visitor Experience: results of research focused on analyzing the perception and impact of scenography's visual elements on visitors, as well as the effectiveness of routes and scenarios.
Keywords: digital scenography, interfaces, augmented reality, virtual reality, machine learning, video projections, installations, audio design, interactivity, personalization of the visitor experience|
